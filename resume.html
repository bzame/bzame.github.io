Graduate Thesis

Title
IRT Model Estimation Via Penalized Likelihood
Supervisor
prof. Nicola Sartori
Description
This thesis is about IRT (Item Response Theory) 1PL and 2PL models, in particular, exploring new estimation methods based on penalized likelihood function with the purpose of improving estimates convergence. With this work, my supervisor and I have shown IRT model estimation via L1-penalized likelihood seems useful to select which coefficients are really significant, even if 2PL models do not belong to glms.

Undergraduate Thesis

Title
AMonteCarloStudyofthePropertiesofaNon-ParametricEstimator for the Area Under the ROC-Curve
Supervisor
prof. Monica Chiogna
Description
Area Under ROC Curve non-parametric estimator properties study via Monte Carlo simulation. The estimator is based on the K nearest neighbor method. This thesis purpose is estimator unbiasedness validation and estimator variance approximation. With this work, my supervisor and I have shown the proposed non-parametric estimator is unbiased and normally distributed. We also proposed a bootstrap approach to estimate its variance.
